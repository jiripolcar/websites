About me

I'm currently a PhD student at the University of West Bohemia, Department of Industrial Engineering and Management, Faculty of Mechanical Engineering. I'm originally an industrial engineer, but during my PhD studies, I've rediscovered my long forgotten passion for programming, thanks to my signature project - the Risk Environment Simulator.

Programming in Unity3D became my hobby. I've been doing it for four years now and learned a lot about it. I attended two hackathons, where I made projects in Unity - 2016 Aimtechackathon in Pilsen, Czechia and 2017 Jamchester gaming jam in Manchester, UK. That's where I found out that I want to continue going down the path of 3D programming.

I'm particularly interested in virtual reality and in it's serious purposes. If you ask me, I believe virtual reality is going to be a huge thing in medicine, education and design, but I'm quite sceptical about industrial applications - don't hesitate to write me, if you want to discuss it.

My PhD studies have given me not only the skill to dig through scientific publication and to write them, it also gave me the ability to speak in front of people and to teach. These are soft skills I had been missing before and I'm thankful for my time spending here because of it.

Sitting all day long in front of computer is, unforunately, a common thing among IT people. That's the same for me, as well as sitting in the car for the rest of the day. This is why I compensate it for my body by eating well and doing sports: mainly long distance running, hiking, bouldering and cycling.

Risk Environment Simulator

As I've written before, this is my signature project and favourite, although it's a tough free-time garage work. It is a support for alcohol addiction treatment. The addicts are being treated in a safe and closed environment of the clinic, where they can use this simulator for training of their behaviour in risky situations, which is exposure to alcoholic beverages. Technically a 3D computer game taking place in a pub has an unbelievably strong effect on people who are addicted to alcohol.

Alcohol addiction is a big problem not only in Czech Republic, but in a lot of countries across the world as well. Although I'm an engineer, I've been learning a lot about these problems and since I've seen an addict using the simluator for the first time, I believe this project has a great potential. Now, the psychiatry part of our team is designing clinical studies to evaluate the effects.

    CloudSlicer

    When I was working at the Regional Technological Institute, I had access to a Leica ScanStation C5 3D scanner and needed to find a fast and simple way to convert the huge point clouds it created into something usable for further industrial engineering purposes. CloudSlicer is a fast tool written in C++ that allows you to quickly slice the point cloud and project these slices to bitmap images. These can be then used as underlay for layout modelling, discrete event simulations, ergonomy analyses etc. The problem with 3D scanning for these purposes is that the industry leading tools are not able to either import the point clouds at all or they become too laggy to work with them. On the other hand, all of these tools can import a bitmap image as an underlay. CloudSlicer includes a point cloud viewer made in Unity3D:

    Layout Maker

    LayoutMaker was my project I crashed into the wall with, although starting a big project without experience gave me many. The problem is that this project was too huge for one person and I didn't have any teammates to help me with it. This was a tool in Unity3D intended for 3D layout modelling. I finished the GUI and scene serialization for saving and loading, some 3D models import as well. With so much work left, I decided to leave the project as it would take decades to finish (something you find out too late if you don't have the experience). Still, I'm glad that I could learn a lot about software projects.
